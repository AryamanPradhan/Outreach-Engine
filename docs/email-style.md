# Cold Email Style Guide

Style: Nick Saraev. Curious, conversational, human. Never salesy.

---

## Structure

```
Subject: [Short subject based on the observation]

Hi Team,

[Specific observation from the company's website or LinkedIn profile]

[Why that observation stood out]

I spend a lot of time helping businesses simplify internal processes and identify opportunities where AI can create practical value.

Curious, [one question about a challenge they likely face based on the observation]?

Open to a quick 15 to 20 min chat sometime next week?

Best,

Aryaman
```

---

## Rules

**Length**
- 80 to 120 words total. Count carefully.

**Opening**
- Always start with "Hi Team,"

**Observation**
- Use only information explicitly found on the company's website or LinkedIn profile.
- Focus on one specific thing that genuinely stands out — a product, a market they serve, a team structure, a recent move, a stated goal.
- Do not make assumptions. Do not fake personalization.

**Question**
- Ask exactly one question.
- Ground it in the observation.
- It should relate to one of: growth, operations, delivery, scaling, client management, technology, or business challenges.

**Tone**
- Curious and conversational. Write like a person, not a pitch deck.
- No hyphens.
- No corporate buzzwords (no "leverage", "synergy", "streamline", "empower", "optimize", "cutting-edge", etc.).
- Do not pitch services.
- Do not promise results.
- Do not mention automation.
- Do not mention saving money.

**Sign-off**
- End with a request for a 15 to 20 minute conversation.
- Sign off as Aryaman.
